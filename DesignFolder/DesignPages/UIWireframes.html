My index page aims for simplicity, featuring just the title of my website and four hyperlinks leading to the main sections. Sometimes, I feel it strikes the right balance, offering a clean and focused entry point for visitors. Yet, there are moments of uncertainty where I wonder if it might be too minimalistic. Despite this, I find myself drawn to its straightforward approach and am inclined to stick with this design for now, appreciating its simplicity.

I am still not sure how I feel about giving users the choice of what they look at. As odd as it may sound, I grapple with the notion of loading users into a website and immediately granting them the freedom to choose their path. There's a part of me that questions whether this approach might dilute the intended experience or lead to a disjointed journey. However, I also recognize the importance of user agency in shaping their interactions with the site and acknowledge its significant impact on overall user experience. Perhaps even offering a simple 'About Me' page as the initial landing point could streamline the user's experience while still providing a sense of autonomy. This is a concept I'm eager to explore further through playtesting and user feedback.



The design for the blog index resonates with me for its simplicity and effectiveness. It's incredibly straightforward yet remarkably functional. The layout isn't overwhelming, making it easy for visitors to locate what they need. The top navigation bar facilitates seamless movement across the website, while the prominent "Weekly Blogs" title in the center draws attention to the latest posts. Beneath it, the neatly listed blog entries maintain clarity and organization, ensuring users can easily find the content they're looking for.


While experimenting with a more modern or "digital" aesthetic for this design iteration, I find myself torn between the allure of visual captivation and the comfort of familiarity. Although visually captivating, I find myself gravitating back towards the simplicity of the first design. There's an undeniable allure to the sleekness of this new approach, yet something about the familiarity of the original design resonates with me.


Inspired by the simplicity of the initial blog index design, I sought to replicate its effectiveness for the essays page. While I aimed to differentiate the two, upon reflection, I realize that consistency throughout the website could enhance the overall user experience. Perhaps incorporating similar elements from the blog index design would create a cohesive browsing experience across different sections of the website, enhancing user engagement and navigation.


My current portfolio page design appeals to me, but I have reservations about its future appearance once I start adding screenshots of my game. The potential for drastic contrasts between elements concerns me, as it may disrupt the overall aesthetic I've cultivated. Despite this uncertainty, I appreciate the overall design and layout of the page, finding it visually appealing and functional.
