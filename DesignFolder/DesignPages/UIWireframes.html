My index page aims for simplicity, featuring just the title of my website and four hyperlinks leading to the main sections. Sometimes, I feel it strikes the right balance, offering a clean and focused entry point for visitors. Yet, there are moments of uncertainty where I wonder if it might be too minimalistic. Despite this, I find myself drawn to its straightforward approach and am inclined to stick with this design for now, appreciating its simplicity.

I am still not sure how I feel about giving users the choice of what they look at. As odd as it may sound, I grapple with the notion of loading users into a website and immediately granting them the freedom to choose their path. There's a part of me that questions whether this approach might dilute the intended experience or lead to a disjointed journey. However, I also recognize the importance of user agency in shaping their interactions with the site and acknowledge its significant impact on overall user experience. Perhaps even offering a simple 'About Me' page as the initial landing point could streamline the user's experience while still providing a sense of autonomy. This is a concept I'm eager to explore further through playtesting and user feedback.

Goal Alignment

The goal of the website design is to create a clean, minimalist aesthetic that focuses on user experience and easy navigation. By keeping a consistent layout with the name and navigation at the top across all pages, the design supports intuitive browsing and helps users quickly find what they need.

Information Structure - Content Mapping

The content on the website is organized into four main sections, each accessible via cards on the index page. This high-level structure provides a clear overview of the site's offerings. Within each section, a list of linked components with hover text descriptions gives users a preview of the content without overwhelming them with too much information upfront.

User Flow - Screens, Behaviour, and Decisions

The user flow starts on the index page, where they can click on one of the four cards to enter a specific section. Each section page follows a similar layout with a heading and list of linked components. When the user hovers over a component, the text changes to reveal more details about that page's content. Clicking on a component takes the user to the corresponding page.

List and explanation of selected Interface Elements

The key interface elements chosen for the website include: Navigation: A simple navigation bar at the top right allows users to easily move between pages. Keeping it consistent across the site enhances usability.
  • Cards: The four cards on the index page serve as entry points to the main sections. Their large size and placement make them visually prominent.
  • Linked Components: The lists of linked components on each section page provide access to the site's content. Hover effects add interactivity and give users a preview of the linked pages.
  • Poppins Font: This clean, readable font contributes to the minimalist aesthetic and ensures content is easy to scan. Minimal Text: By limiting text on pages and using hover effects for additional details, the design avoids clutter and focuses the user's attention on the most important information.

These interface elements work together to create a user-friendly experience that is easy to navigate and visually appealing. The consistent layout and use of interactive elements like hover effects enhance engagement and help users find the content they need quickly.
