Essay 1: UX & UI Analysis


When tasked with analysing the user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) of South African websites, I embarked on a search for platforms that piqued my interest. Given my inclination for CSS in web design, I was eager to discover sites that not only captivated visually but also excelled in functionality. However, my initial exploration left me underwhelmed, as many websites adhered to conventional design paradigms, offering predictable layouts with standard navigation menus. Despite my lack of immediate attraction to these websites, I couldn't deny their effectiveness in achieving their intended goals. Instead of settling for a superficial examination, I opted for a more insightful approach. I decided to compare two South African websites – one that I deemed "well done" and another that I considered "poorly executed" – to discern the factors contributing to a desirable UX. For this analysis, I chose News24's website as an exemplar of effective design and Eskom's website as a case study for UX shortcomings. To commence this comparative assessment, I utilized the Interaction Design (IxD) process along with other independent resources to evaluate how each website aligned with its objectives and whether it successfully met user needs.



News24, one of South Africa's premier digital news platforms, stands as a testament to exemplary UI/UX design. Upon landing on the website, users are greeted with a clean and intuitive interface. The homepage strikes a balance between showcasing breaking news headlines and providing easy access to various sections such as politics, sports, entertainment, and business. The layout is thoughtfully organized, with prominent navigation elements guiding users to explore different topics or access specific content swiftly. News articles are presented in a visually appealing manner, with high-quality images and concise summaries that entice users to delve deeper into stories. One of the standout features of News24's UX is its responsiveness across devices. Whether accessed on a desktop, tablet, or smartphone, the website seamlessly adjusts its layout to provide an optimal viewing experience. This adaptability enhances user engagement and ensures accessibility for a diverse audience. Furthermore, News24 prioritizes user interaction by incorporating features such as comment sections and social media sharing buttons, empowering users to participate in discussions and share content with their networks. These interactive elements foster a sense of community and contribute to the overall richness of the user experience. In terms of ethics in interaction design, News24 demonstrates a commitment to transparency and integrity in its content delivery. The platform adheres to journalistic principles of accuracy, objectivity, and accountability, thereby establishing trust with its audience. As Ward puts it, "Journalistic integrity, characterized by accuracy, objectivity, and accountability, is fundamental for building trust and credibility in media organizations, fostering positive relationships with audiences." (Ward, 2018, p. 56). Additionally, the website's advertising practices are unobtrusive, ensuring that user experience remains paramount without compromising on revenue generation. Overall, News24 sets a benchmark for UI/UX excellence in the South African digital landscape, embodying principles of usability, accessibility, and ethical design.



In stark contrast to News24's user-centric approach, Eskom's website exemplifies the pitfalls of subpar UI/UX design. As South Africa's primary electricity provider, Eskom's website serves as a crucial resource for customers seeking information on services, tariffs, and power outages.



However, upon visiting the website, users are confronted with a cluttered and confusing interface that create a heavy visual weight and detracts from the overall user experience. The homepage is inundated with an overwhelming amount of text, images, and links, making it challenging for technologically inexperienced users to discern the most relevant information. Given the diverse audience of the website, accessibility should be a primary focus to ensure everyone can access the information seamlessly. As Jones and Patel highlight, "Accessibility ensures that digital platforms are usable by individuals with diverse abilities, including those with disabilities or limited technological literacy" (Jones & Patel, 2020, p. 78). Navigation is a major pain point on Eskom's website, with complex menus and buried content hindering users' ability to find what they need efficiently. As Hick’s Law states, “The time taken for a decision increases with the number and complexity of choices” (Goldberg, 2024, p. 12). In the case of Eskom, Important features, such as accessing account information or reporting faults, are buried within complicated submenus, leading to frustration and disengagement. Furthermore, Eskom's website suffers from poor responsiveness and optimization for mobile devices. Pages load slowly, and the layout often breaks, causing inconvenience for users accessing the site on smartphones or tablets. This lack of mobile friendliness worsens usability issues and push away a significant portion of the audience. Ethically, Eskom's website raises concerns regarding transparency and accessibility. Critical information, such as load shedding schedules or energy-saving tips, is not readily accessible and may be buried within obscure sections of the website. This opacity undermines trust and impairs the ability of users to make informed decisions about their energy consumption. In summary, Eskom's website serves as a cautionary tale of the consequences of neglecting UI/UX principles. Its shortcomings in design, navigation, and transparency detract from the overall user experience and highlight the importance of prioritizing user needs and ethical considerations in interaction design. Comparative Analysis: Insights and Reflections



Comparing News24 and Eskom's websites underscores the pivotal role of UI/UX design in shaping user perceptions and interactions. While News24 excels in providing a seamless and engaging user experience, Eskom's website falls short in meeting user expectations and ethical standards. As Donald Norman states, "User-centric design principles prioritize the needs, preferences, and behaviours of users throughout the design process, ensuring that digital products and services are tailored to meet their expectations" (Norman, 2013, p. 29). Several key factors contribute to the divergent outcomes observed in these case studies:




  • Usability: News24 prioritizes simplicity and intuitiveness in its design, facilitating easy navigation and content discovery. In contrast, Eskom's website suffers from complexity and clutter, impeding users' ability to accomplish tasks efficiently.

  • Accessibility: News24 demonstrates a commitment to accessibility by ensuring responsiveness across devices and incorporating features that accommodate diverse user needs. Conversely, Eskom's website fails to prioritize mobile optimization and may exclude users with disabilities or limited technological literacy.

  • Transparency: News24 fosters trust through transparent content delivery and ethical advertising practices. Eskom's website, however, lacks transparency in its communication of critical information, leading to user frustration and distrust.
