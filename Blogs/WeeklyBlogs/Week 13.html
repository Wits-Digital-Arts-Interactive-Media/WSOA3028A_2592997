Ethical Internet

To me, the internet as an ethical space means that the online world should be a place where we uphold and promote ethical principles and values. This includes ensuring that the internet is a safe, inclusive, and equitable environment for all users, where fundamental human rights and freedoms are respected and protected.

In an ideal wordl, I would envision a space where all individuals are treated with respect, dignity, and without discrimination, regardless of their background, identity, or beliefs. Personal data and privacy are safeguarded, and users have control over how their information is collected, used, and shared. Individuals can freely express their thoughts and opinions, while also being mindful of the potential impact on others and the need to avoid harm or hate speech.

There are clear and transparent processes in place to address issues, such as online harassment, misinformation, or abuse, with appropriate accountability measures for those who violate ethical standards. The internet is designed to be accessible and inclusive, catering to the diverse needs and abilities of all users, including those with disabilities or from marginalized communities.

However, achieving this vision of an ethical internet is not without its challenges. Navigating the complex interplay between individual rights, such as freedom of expression, and the need to prevent harm or protect vulnerable groups can be challenging. The pace of technological change, with the emergence of new platforms, algorithms, and data-driven technologies, can outpace the development of ethical frameworks and governance structures.

Establishing consistent ethical standards and enforcement mechanisms across different jurisdictions and cultural contexts can be a significant challenge, requiring global cooperation and coordination. The widespread dissemination of false or misleading information online can undermine trust, erode democratic processes, and pose serious ethical and societal challenges.

Reflection on development process

This week, my focus was on refining the visual aspects of my website, particularly by adding an introductory animation. I aimed to create an engaging experience for users with text dynamically appearing on the screen, welcoming them to my website. However, I encountered a significant challenge stemming from my background in programming, particularly in Unity. Transitioning to web design revealed stark differences in how content is structured and accessed.

In Unity, creating distinct scenes and transitioning between them is straightforward, but in web development, the paradigm is fundamentally different. The index page serves as the main entry point, which initially puzzled me. My initial approach involved creating a full-screen element that would simulate a separate page, gradually revealing its content upon user interaction. However, I soon realized that each page load triggered a reload of associated scripts, complicating my efforts to maintain the state of this element across page transitions.

Despite these hurdles, I've embraced the learning opportunity this challenge presented. Delving deeper into web development, I've explored concepts such as local storage, cookies, and event listeners. These investigations have broadened my understanding of web technologies, even though implementing them remains a work in progress. Currently, I'm still fine-tuning the functionality, striving to achieve the desired outcome.
