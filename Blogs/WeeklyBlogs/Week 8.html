Birhane and Cummins - 2019 - Algorithmic Injustices Towards a Relational Ethic

Technologies developed for the 'centre' disproportionately impact the 'margins'. This phenomenon becomes even more pronounced when these technologies are created, tested, and implemented by predominantly white men from the US. The question then arises: what happens to everyone else?

When tech development is centralized around a specific demographic, particularly white men from the US, it inherently carries their biases, perspectives, and experiences. This consistency in the tech industry leads to a lack of diversity in thought, resulting in products and systems that may not adequately cater to the needs and realities of diverse populations worldwide. As a consequence, marginalized communities, including women, people of colour, and individuals from different cultural backgrounds, face the brunt of these technological innovations.

One of the primary issues stemming from this imbalance is the perpetuation of societal inequalities and biases. Algorithms and AI systems, for example, often reflect the biases of their creators, leading to discriminatory outcomes in areas such as hiring practices, predictive policing, healthcare, and more. These technologies, when deployed without considering the diverse perspectives and experiences of marginalized groups, can exacerbate existing disparities and further marginalize those already at the fringes of society.

Moreover, the lack of representation in tech development not only limits the scope of innovation but also hinders the potential for inclusive and equitable solutions. By excluding diverse voices from the creation and implementation of technology, we miss out on valuable insights, alternative viewpoints, and unique problem-solving approaches that could address the complex challenges faced by different communities.

To address these issues, it is imperative to promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in the tech industry. By fostering a more inclusive environment that values and amplifies diverse voices, we can ensure that technological advancements benefit all members of society, not just a privileged few. Encouraging collaboration across different backgrounds, cultures, and experiences can lead to the development of more ethical, inclusive, and socially responsible technologies that uplift and empower marginalized communities.

In conclusion, the impact of technologies developed by the 'centre' on the 'margins' underscores the urgent need for a more inclusive and diverse approach to tech development. By recognizing and addressing the biases inherent in current tech practices, we can pave the way for a more equitable and just technological landscape that benefits everyone, regardless of their background or identity.

Weekly Reflection

Throughout this week, I've come to appreciate the profound importance of UI wireframes in the web development process. Previously, I might have considered them as optional or merely a preliminary step, but now I realize their crucial role in shaping the user experience and guiding development. These wireframes serve as blueprints for the entire project, providing a visual representation of the layout, structure, and functionality of the website. They enable stakeholders to visualize the final product early on, allowing for valuable feedback before significant time and resources are invested. Integrating UI wireframes into my workflow this week had a significant impact on my development process. With a clear roadmap of the website's design and functionality, I worked more efficiently, focusing efforts on implementing features and refining the user interface based on wireframe guidelines. This experience taught me that wireframes are not just design tools but communication tools, facilitating collaboration and alignment among project stakeholders. They ensure everyone shares a common vision and understanding of the final product, leading to a more cohesive and successful outcome. Moving forward, I plan to continue incorporating UI wireframes from the outset, streamlining development, reducing misunderstandings, and delivering websites that are visually appealing, intuitive, and user-friendly.
