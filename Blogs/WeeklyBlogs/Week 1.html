As We May Think - Vannevar Bush 1945

Reflecting on Vannevar Bush's essay "As We May Think," it's pretty amazing how he saw the future of tech way back in 1945. He talked about this thing called the MEMEX that could totally change how we access information, kind of like what we do with the internet now.

It's actually crazy how ahead of his time he was. And what's even crazier is that he has a background in nuclear research which adds this interesting twist to his ideas, showing how technology can be both a force for good and a bit scary at the same time. Overall, the reading makes you think about how far we've come with tech and how it's shaping our world today.

I also find it pretty cool to see how his ideas from way back still make a lot of sense in how we use the internet today. He talked about how important it is for information systems to work the way our brains do, focusing on how we naturally connect ideas instead of forcing us into strict categories.

We see this in how websites recommend stuff to you based on what you've looked at before. That's like what Bush was talking about with his idea of personalizing information based on our experiences. It's like having a friend who knows exactly what you like and suggests things you might be into.

Something I found interesting about the WWW

One really cool thing about the history of the World Wide Web the fact that it all started with a simple idea from Tim Berners-Lee. He came up with this idea of using hypertext to manage information within the organization. Basically, he wanted to create a network of connected documents that anyone could access from their computer. And now from that small idea, we've got the internet as we know it today, changing the way we share and find information across the globe. It's pretty amazing how something so simple turned into something so big.
